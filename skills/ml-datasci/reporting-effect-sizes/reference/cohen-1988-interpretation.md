# Cohen 1988 small/medium/large conventions — and why they are conventions

The famous Cohen 1988 thresholds are CONVENTIONS adopted for psychology research where domain knowledge often did not exist to set thresholds empirically. Cohen himself warned against treating them as universal.

## The thresholds

| Effect-size metric | Small | Medium | Large |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cohen's d (and dz) | 0.2 | 0.5 | 0.8 |
| Cliff's delta | 0.147 | 0.33 | 0.474 |
| Pearson's r | 0.1 | 0.3 | 0.5 |
| R² | 0.01 | 0.09 | 0.25 |
| Partial eta-squared | 0.01 | 0.06 | 0.14 |
| Odds ratio | 1.5 | 2.5 | 4.0 |

## Why these are conventions, not laws

A d = 0.2 might be:

- **Trivially small** in a context where the cost of intervention is high and the population baseline is already healthy (e.g., a public-health intervention that reduces population BP by 0.2 SDs — meaningful at population scale, irrelevant for an individual decision)
- **Massive** in a context where the baseline is "nothing works" and any improvement matters (e.g., a treatment for an otherwise-untreatable disease)
- **Practically zero** in a high-stakes safety context where d = 0.5 is the minimum-detectable-effect that would change practice

## When to cite the conventions anyway

Cite them as ONE input, not the whole story:

> Cohen's d = 0.49 (95% CI: 0.12, 0.86). Cohen 1988 conventions label this a medium effect. In the BP-treatment context, a 4.3 mmHg systolic decrease is clinically meaningful for borderline hypertensive patients (where lifetime cardiovascular risk shifts noticeably) but trivial for normotensive patients.

The convention sets a baseline expectation; the domain context sets the actual interpretation.

## When NOT to cite the conventions

Skip them entirely when:

- The domain has its own thresholds (e.g., clinical guidelines define MCID — minimum clinically important difference)
- The audience is a domain-specific journal with established norms (some psychology subfields have moved past Cohen 1988)
- The effect size is so large or so small that the convention adds no information

## What to write instead of "this is a large effect"

- **Domain-anchored**: "Cohen's d = 1.4 — larger than the 0.5 MCID established for this patient population (Smith 2019)."
- **Compared to baseline**: "Cohen's d = 0.6 — comparable to the effect sizes typically seen for behavioral interventions in this population (range 0.3–0.7, Jones 2021 meta-analysis)."
- **Practical units**: "Mean difference = 4.3 mmHg — within the 3–5 mmHg range that prior trials have associated with reduced stroke incidence in this population."

The general rule: prefer DOMAIN context over Cohen 1988 labels. If domain context is unavailable, cite the convention with an explicit caveat that it is a convention.
